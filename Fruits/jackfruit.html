Jackfruit - The Gentle Giant

The largest tree-borne fruit in the world with incredible versatility

About Jackfruit

Jackfruit (Artocarpus heterophyllus) is native to South India and is the largest tree-borne fruit in the world, weighing up to 55 kg (120 pounds). It belongs to the Moraceae family, which also includes figs and mulberries.


Known as "Kathal" in Hindi, "Panasa" in Telugu, "Palaa" in Tamil, and "Chakka" in Malayalam, jackfruit has been cultivated in India for thousands of years. Its unique feature is its versatility - it can be consumed at different stages of ripeness and serves as both a fruit and a vegetable.

Varieties of Jackfruit in India

  • Rudrakshi - Known for its small to medium size and sweet taste
  • Singapore - Features crisp and sweet flesh with excellent quality
  • Burliar - Noted for its high yield and good quality
  • PLR-1 - A variety developed at Tamil Nadu Agricultural University
  • Muttam Varikka - From Kerala, known for its exceptional sweetness
  • Sindhoor - Features small-sized, sweet bulbs with excellent quality

Nutritional Benefits

Jackfruit is a nutritional powerhouse. It contains:

  • Rich in antioxidants and phytonutrients
  • Good source of vitamin C and B-complex vitamins
  • High in potassium, magnesium, and manganese
  • Contains dietary fiber for digestive health
  • Low in calories while being filling and satisfying
  • Contains protein, making it unique among fruits

Due to its fibrous texture when unripe, jackfruit has gained popularity as a meat substitute for vegetarians and vegans, often used in dishes like "jackfruit pulled pork."

Cultural Significance

In India, jackfruit holds cultural importance:

  • It is the state fruit of Kerala and Tamil Nadu
  • The jackfruit tree is considered sacred in some regions
  • In folklore, it's believed that planting a jackfruit tree brings prosperity to the family
  • During traditional festivals in South India, jackfruit is often included in offerings to deities
  • The wood of the jackfruit tree is prized for furniture making and musical instruments

Culinary Uses

Jackfruit is extremely versatile in Indian cuisine:

  • Ripe Jackfruit - Enjoyed fresh as a sweet, juicy fruit
  • Jackfruit Chips - Thin slices of unripe jackfruit fried and salted
  • Kathal ki Sabzi - A North Indian curry made with unripe jackfruit
  • Chakka Varatti - A jackfruit jam from Kerala
  • Jackfruit Payasam - A sweet pudding made with ripe jackfruit
  • Phanas Poli - A Maharashtrian sweet flatbread stuffed with jackfruit
  • Jackfruit Biryani - Rice dish where jackfruit replaces meat

The seeds are also edible and are often boiled or roasted and consumed as a snack or added to curries.

Growing Season

In India, jackfruit is typically in season from March to June, with peak availability in April and May. However, in some regions with suitable climates, jackfruit can be available for a longer period.

Interesting Facts

  • A single jackfruit can feed multiple families due to its enormous size
  • The sticky latex from jackfruit was traditionally used as glue
  • Jackfruit trees can produce up to 200 fruits per year
  • The aroma of ripe jackfruit is strong and distinctive, sometimes described as a combination of pineapple, banana, and mango
  • Jackfruit is sometimes called the "miracle fruit" for its potential to address food security in tropical regions
