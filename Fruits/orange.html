Orange - The Vitamin C Citrus

A vibrant citrus fruit treasured for its refreshing taste and health benefits

About Orange

Orange (Citrus sinensis) is a popular citrus fruit known for its bright color, juicy segments, and refreshing taste. Native to ancient China, oranges spread across the globe through trade routes and are now cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide, including significant production in India.


In India, oranges are known by various names including "Santra" in Hindi, "Kamala" in Bengali, "Naranga" in Malayalam, and "Mosambi" (sweet lime variety) in many parts of the country. They are an important commercial crop in states like Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, Assam, Nagpur, and the Northeastern regions.

Varieties of Orange in India

  • Nagpur Orange - Famous for its bright color and sweet-tart flavor, also known as Nagpur Santra
  • Darjeeling Orange - Grown in the hills of West Bengal, known for its unique flavor
  • Khasi Mandarin - Popular in Northeast India, particularly Meghalaya
  • Coorg Orange - Cultivated in the Western Ghats region of Karnataka
  • Sathgudi - A popular variety in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana
  • Mosambi - Sweet lime variety widely consumed across India
  • Blood Orange - Less common variety with red-tinged flesh cultivated in some cooler regions

Nutritional Benefits

Oranges are nutritional powerhouses:

  • Exceptionally high in vitamin C, essential for immune function
  • Good source of dietary fiber, particularly in the membrane between segments
  • Contains folate, important for cell division and DNA synthesis
  • Rich in antioxidants like flavonoids and carotenoids
  • Provides potassium, supporting heart health and blood pressure regulation
  • Contains vitamin B1, B2, and calcium
  • Low in calories and high in water content
  • Contains citric acid which aids in digestion

In Ayurveda, oranges are considered to pacify Vata and Kapha doshas while potentially increasing Pitta, making them cooling in summer but warming in winter.

Cultural Significance

Oranges hold special significance in Indian culture:

  • In Hindu traditions, orange fruits are often offered to deities during rituals
  • The Nagpur Orange Festival celebrates the harvest with cultural programs and exhibitions
  • Oranges are considered auspicious gifts during festive seasons
  • Orange peels are used in traditional incense making
  • The color orange (derived from the fruit) has spiritual significance in Indian culture
  • Many temple prasadams (offerings) include orange segments

Culinary Uses

Oranges versatility extends to many culinary applications:

  • Fresh Consumption - Eaten as segments or juiced
  • Orange Juice - Popular breakfast beverage and mixer
  • Marmalades - Traditional preserves using the peel and juice
  • Santra Barfi - An Indian sweet made with orange juice
  • Orange Rasam - A South Indian soup with tangy orange notes
  • Marinades - The acidic juice tenderizes meats
  • Orange Peel Candy - Crystallized orange peels as a sweet treat
  • Kulfi and Ice Creams - Orange-flavored frozen desserts
  • Chutneys - Combined with spices for tangy condiments

The zest (outer colored layer of peel) is prized for its aromatic oils that add flavor to many dishes and baked goods.

Growing Season

In India, oranges have two main growing seasons. The main harvest is from November to February (winter crop), while some regions produce a smaller summer crop from April to June. Nagpur oranges are typically at their best during winter months, making them a popular fruit during the festive season.

Interesting Facts

  • Oranges are actually a hybrid between pomelo and mandarin
  • The word "orange" comes from the Sanskrit word "naranga"
  • India is among the top ten orange-producing countries globally
  • A fully grown orange tree can produce up to 500 fruits per year
  • Orange blossoms are highly fragrant and used in perfumery
  • The white pith between the peel and fruit contains high levels of pectin and flavonoids
  • Nagpur is often called the "Orange City" due to its famous orange cultivation
  • Orange oil extracted from the peel is used in aromatherapy and cleaning products
