Mango - The King of Fruits

India's national fruit and a symbol of love, friendship and prosperity

About Mango

Mango (Mangifera indica) is known as the "King of Fruits" in India and is the national fruit of India, Pakistan, and the Philippines. India is the largest producer of mangoes in the world, cultivating over 1,000 varieties, each with its unique flavor, aroma, and texture.


Mangoes have been cultivated in India for over 4,000 years and hold a special place in Indian culture, mythology, and religion. The fruit is often associated with prosperity, abundance, and good fortune.

Popular Indian Mango Varieties

  • Alphonso (Hapus) - From Maharashtra, known for its rich, creamy texture and sweet flavor
  • Dasheri - From Uttar Pradesh, recognized for its sweet taste and distinctive aroma
  • Langra - From Bihar and eastern Uttar Pradesh, known for its fibrous texture and sweet-tangy flavor
  • Chausa - From northern India, appreciated for its sweet juice and smooth texture
  • Kesar - From Gujarat, renowned for its saffron color and sweet taste
  • Banganapalli - From Andhra Pradesh, known for its sweet flavor and fiber-free pulp
  • Himsagar - From West Bengal, treasured for its sweet, aromatic pulp
  • Totapuri - From South India, recognized for its distinct beak-like shape and tangy flavor

Nutritional Benefits

Mangoes are not just delicious but also highly nutritious. They are rich in:

  • Vitamins A, C, and E
  • Potassium
  • Antioxidants (especially beta-carotene)
  • Dietary fiber
  • Folate

Regular consumption of mangoes may support eye health, boost immunity, improve digestion, and promote healthy skin.

Cultural Significance

In India, the mango tree and its fruit hold immense cultural significance:

  • Mango leaves are used as decoration during weddings and religious ceremonies as they symbolize prosperity
  • The tree is associated with Kama, the god of love
  • Mango motifs are commonly found in Indian art, textiles, and architecture
  • Annual mango festivals are held across India to celebrate different varieties
  • Gifting mangoes is a tradition during the summer season in many Indian households

Culinary Uses

Mangoes are incredibly versatile in Indian cuisine:

  • Aam Ras - Mango pulp enjoyed as a sweet dish with puris
  • Mango Pickle (Aam ka Achaar) - A spicy condiment made from raw mangoes
  • Mango Lassi - A yogurt-based drink flavored with mango pulp
  • Mango Chutney - A sweet and spicy condiment made from ripe mangoes
  • Aam Panna - A refreshing summer drink made from raw mangoes
  • Mango Rice - A savory dish made with rice and raw mangoes
  • Mango Desserts - Kulfi, ice cream, mousse, and other sweets

Growing Season

In India, mangoes are typically in season from March to July, depending on the variety and region. The peak season is usually May to June when most varieties are available in abundance.
